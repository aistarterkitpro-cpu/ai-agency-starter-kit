# AI Agency Pricing Guide
## How to Price, Package & Profit from AI Services

---

## The #1 Pricing Mistake New Agencies Make

Charging by the hour. Stop. AI work is leverage — you can deliver in 2 hours what used to take 20. Hour-based pricing punishes your efficiency. **Charge by value, not time.**

---

## Value-Based Pricing Framework

Ask yourself (and your client) these questions before quoting:
1. What is this problem costing them monthly? (missed leads, staff time, lost revenue)
2. What would solving it be worth annually?
3. What's a fair share of that value to charge?

**Rule of thumb:** Charge 10–20% of the annual value you create.

> Example: A restaurant misses 40 calls/week. Average order = $35. That's $72,800/year in missed revenue. Your AI receptionist captures even 25% of that = $18,200/year saved. Charge $1,500–$3,000 setup + $300/mo retainer. Total year 1: $5,100. That's less than 30% of value created. Easy sell.

## The Retainer Model (Your Path to $5k+/Month)

**Target:** 10 clients at $500/mo = $5,000/mo recurring

**How to convert one-time to retainer:**
- At delivery: "I can monitor and optimize this for you ongoing for $297/mo."
- 30 days later: "I noticed 3 things we could improve — want me on a monthly plan?"

## Payment Terms
- Always take 50% upfront minimum
- Net-15 for retainers (invoice 1st, due 15th)
- Late fee: 5% after 30 days
- Never discount more than 20%, only in exchange for something (upfront pay, longer contract)

---

## Pricing Psychology
- **Anchor high.** Quote Pro first. Let them land on Growth. They feel like they won.
- **Odd numbers convert.** $497 > $500.
- **Show the math.** "This pays for itself if you close 2 extra clients."
- **Never apologize for your price.** Confidence = credibility.
