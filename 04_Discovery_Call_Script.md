# Discovery Call Script
### For AI Agency Sales Calls — From Opener to Close

---

**How to Use This:**
Read through this before every call until the structure is in your bones. After 10 calls, you won't need the script — but you'll use the framework forever. Feel free to adapt language to match your style. The structure matters more than the exact words.

**Call length target:** 30–45 minutes
**Goal:** Understand their pain, connect it to your solution, and book next steps or close.

---

## Pre-Call Preparation (5 min before)

Before every call, spend 5 minutes doing this:

- [ ] Look up their Google reviews — any complaints about slow response, missed calls, or communication?
- [ ] Check their website — do they have a chat widget? Contact form? Clear pricing?
- [ ] Look at their social media — how often are they posting? What's getting engagement?
- [ ] Note 1–2 specific things you can reference on the call to show you did your homework

**Write down:**
- Their biggest obvious problem (what you noticed):
- The service you think fits best:
- The pricing tier you'll lead with:

---

## Part 1: Opening (2–3 min)

**Start warm, not scripted.**

> "Hey [Name], thanks for making time — I know you're busy. Before I jump into anything, I just want to make sure this call is actually useful for you.
>
> What I'd love to do is spend the first 10–15 minutes just understanding your business and what's going on with [general area: lead generation / customer communication / your team's workload]. Then I can tell you honestly whether what I do is even relevant. Fair?"

**Why this works:** It sets a consultative tone, removes pressure, and signals that you're not going to pitch aggressively.

---

## Part 2: Rapport and Context (3–5 min)

Ask 1–2 warm-up questions before going deep. Pick from:

- "How long have you been running [Business Name]? Give me the quick backstory."
- "How'd you originally get into [industry]?"
- "What does a typical week look like for you right now?"

**Listen for:** Are they the decision-maker? Are they overwhelmed? Are they growth-focused or stability-focused?

---

## Part 3: Qualifying Questions (10–15 min)

These are your most important questions. Go slowly. Let them talk. Don't interrupt. The more they talk, the more you learn — and the more they trust you.

---

### About Their Current Situation

**"Walk me through how you currently handle [new inquiries / booking / lead follow-up]. What does that process look like?"**

*Listen for: Manual processes, time delays, dropped balls, who's doing it, how often it breaks down.*

---

**"What happens when someone reaches out after hours or on weekends?"**

*Listen for: Missed leads, next-day callbacks, voicemail black holes.*

---

**"How many new leads or inquiries would you say you get in a typical week — ballpark?"**

*Listen for: Volume. Low volume = different conversation than high volume.*

---

**"And of those, how many actually convert into customers or appointments?"**

*Listen for: Conversion problems. Often they don't know the answer — that itself is useful data.*

---

### About Their Pain

**"What's the most frustrating part of [this process] for you right now?"**

*This is the golden question. Stay quiet after asking. Count to 5 if needed. Let them fill the silence.*

---

**"Has that cost you anything — in time, revenue, or stress?"**

*You want them to put a number or feeling on the pain. This becomes the anchor for your value conversation later.*

---

**"Have you tried solving this before? What happened?"**

*Listen for: Prior attempts (tools they tried, agency they hired, hires they made). Understand why those failed.*

---

### About Their Goals

**"If we could fix [the problem they named], what would that actually mean for your business?"**

---

**"What would 'great' look like 6 months from now?"**

---

**"Is growth the priority right now, or more about efficiency and getting your time back?"**

*This tells you whether to frame ROI around revenue or time savings.*

---

### About Decision-Making

**"Is this something you'd be making the call on, or is there a partner or team member who'd need to weigh in?"**

*If there's another decision-maker: "Would it make sense to have [them] on the next call?"*

---

**"Do you have any budget allocated for this kind of solution, or is that TBD?"**

*(Ask this matter-of-factly. Not apologetically. If they push back, say: "Totally fine if it's TBD — I just want to make sure I'm showing you the right options.")*

---

## Part 4: The Pivot — Connect Pain to Solution (5–7 min)

**Summarize what you heard before presenting anything.**

> "Okay, let me make sure I've got this right. [Reflect back their pain in their words:]
>
> It sounds like the main challenge is [X — e.g., 'you're missing a lot of after-hours leads because nobody's monitoring the inbox'] and that's costing you roughly [Y — time, money, stress they mentioned]. And the goal is [Z — e.g., 'to have a more consistent pipeline without adding headcount']. Is that fair?"

*Wait for confirmation. If they correct you, adjust. This is your most important moment — it shows you were listening.*

---

**Then transition:**

> "Based on what you've shared, there are really two things that stand out to me. The first is [primary problem]. The second is [secondary problem if applicable].
>
> What I've built for businesses in similar situations is [describe solution in plain English — NOT feature-heavy]. Let me show you how it works."

---

## Part 5: Presenting the Solution (5–8 min)

**Rule: Never present more than 2 options. Present the one that fits best, then offer an alternative.**

> "For a business like yours, the thing that makes the most sense is [SERVICE + TIER]. Here's what that looks like in practice..."

Walk through:
1. What the system does (in plain English)
2. What it doesn't require from them
3. What a similar client experienced after setup (use a real or composite example)
4. Timeline to go live

**Do NOT read from a slide deck or pricing sheet during this.** Talk to them like a human. The PDF can follow in an email.

---

## Part 6: Handling Objections

---

### Objection: "I need to think about it."

> "Totally fair — I'd never want you to rush into anything. Can I ask what specifically you want to think through? Sometimes there's something I haven't answered clearly and I'd rather address that now."

*(Usually this uncovers the real objection: price, trust, timing, or needing another person's input.)*

---

### Objection: "It's too expensive."

> "I hear you. Can I ask — too expensive relative to what? Like, is it that the price point is beyond budget right now, or does it feel like the value isn't there for what you'd be getting?"

*(If it's budget:)*
> "Let me show you our entry-level option — it gets you [core value] at [Starter price]. The setup is simpler, but it solves the core problem we talked about."

*(If it's value:)*
> "Fair enough — let me reframe this. You mentioned you're losing about [X leads/week] to [this problem]. If we recover even half of those, at your average transaction value of [Y], that's [Z/month] in additional revenue. The system costs [price]. Does it look different when you frame it that way?"

---

### Objection: "We're already using [tool/solution]."

> "Good to know — what are you using? [Listen.] And is it solving the problem you described, or is there still a gap?"

*(Most of the time they're using something basic that isn't actually solving the problem. Help them see that.)*

---

### Objection: "Can you just send me more info?"

> "Sure — I'm happy to do that. I just want to make sure what I send is relevant to your situation. Based on what you've told me, I'd send you [specific thing]. Does that sound right? And when do you think you'd have a chance to look at it? I want to follow up at the right time."

*(Always pin down a follow-up time before hanging up.)*

---

### Objection: "I'm not sure AI is right for us yet."

> "That's a totally reasonable place to be. Can I ask what your hesitation is specifically — is it trust in the technology, uncertainty about the ROI, or something else?"

*(Address the specific fear. Then:)*
> "The businesses I work with usually feel the same way before they see it in action. Would it help to just see a demo of what it actually does? Zero commitment — just 10 minutes and you'll know if it's even relevant."

---

## Part 7: The Close (3–5 min)

**After presenting the solution, transition to close:**

> "So based on everything — does this feel like it addresses what you described? Do you have any questions about what I've outlined?"

*(Handle questions.)*

---

**If they're ready to move forward:**

> "Great. The way I like to start is [describe your kickoff process — e.g., 'I'll send over a short agreement and a first invoice for the setup fee. Once that's signed and processed, we book your kickoff call and we're off'].
>
> I can have that in your inbox within the hour. Sound good?"

---

**If they need more time:**

> "No problem at all. What I'll do is send you a summary of what we talked about and the specific option I'd recommend for you, along with pricing.
>
> When is a good time to reconnect — maybe [2–3 days from now]? I want to make sure you're not sitting on questions."

*(Always book the follow-up before hanging up. "I'll follow up sometime next week" means it never happens.)*

---

## Part 8: After the Call (Do This Within 30 Minutes)

Send a follow-up email with:

**Subject:** Summary from our call + next steps

> Hey [Name],
>
> Really enjoyed talking with you today. Quick recap of what we covered:
>
> **The challenge:** [1–2 sentences reflecting their pain in their words]
>
> **What I'd recommend:** [SERVICE TIER] — [1–2 sentence description]
>
> **Investment:** Setup fee of $[X] + $[Y]/month
>
> **Timeline:** Typically live within [X business days] of kickoff
>
> I've attached [link to services menu / proposal / contract] for your reference.
>
> I'll plan to follow up on [specific date/time we agreed to]. If anything comes up before then, my phone is [number].
>
> Looking forward to working together,
> [Your Name]

## Call Quality Self-Assessment

After each call, score yourself:

| Question | Score (1–5) |
|----------|-------------|
| Did I let them talk more than me? | |
| Did I uncover a specific pain with a dollar or time value? | |
| Did I present a solution tailored to what they said? | |
| Did I ask for the business or a clear next step? | |
| Did I send a follow-up within 30 min? | |

**Total out of 25.** If you're below 18 consistently, the problem is usually question 1 (talking too much) or question 3 (pitching before listening).
